"I love minimalism, of course."
It’s not just about having fewer things — it’s about giving every element a purpose. Minimalism is clarity disguised as simplicity. It’s the discipline of removing noise so the essentials can breathe. When you strip away the unnecessary, what’s left becomes sharper, more intentional, more honest. Minimalism isn’t empty. It’s focused. It’s the confidence to let a single detail carry the moment. And once you start seeing the beauty in restraint, it becomes second nature — almost inevitable. That’s why I say I love minimalism of course — because once you understand it, it stops being a style and becomes a mindset.
